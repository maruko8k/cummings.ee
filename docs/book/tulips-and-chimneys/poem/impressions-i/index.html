Impressions I

the sky a silver
dissonance by the correct
fingers of April
resolved

into a
clutter of trite jewels

 

now like a moth with stumbling

wings flutters and flops along the
grass collides with trees and
houses and finally,
butts into the river

The poem was first published in “Tulips and Chimneys.”

It is also available in json and text formats.
